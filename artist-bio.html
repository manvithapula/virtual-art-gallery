Meet the Artists

Rabindranath Tagore

Rabindranath Tagore (1861-1941) was a Bengali poet, philosopher, artist, and playwright who reshaped Indian literature and music. He is renowned for his poetry collection "Gitanjali," which earned him the Nobel Prize for Literature in 1913. His work "Mother India" reflects his deep national pride and cultural heritage, showcasing his ability to convey profound emotions and social issues through art.

Raja Ravi Varma

Raja Ravi Varma (1848-1906) was a celebrated Indian painter and artist known for his depictions of Hindu deities and mythological themes. His painting "Woman Holding a Fruit" exemplifies his skill in capturing the grace and elegance of traditional Indian womanhood. Varma’s works played a crucial role in popularizing Indian art and culture during the British colonial era.

Vincent van Gogh

Vincent van Gogh (1853-1890) was a Dutch Post-Impressionist painter whose work had a significant influence on 20th-century art. His painting "A Wheatfield with Cypresses" is a notable example of his vibrant use of color and expressive brushstrokes, reflecting his emotional and psychological depth. Van Gogh's work is celebrated for its innovative techniques and powerful visual impact.

Pablo Picasso

Pablo Picasso (1881-1973) was a Spanish painter, sculptor, and one of the most influential artists of the 20th century. Known for co-founding the Cubist movement, his work "Jacqueline with Flowers" represents his unique approach to form and color. Picasso's innovative style and prolific output have left an enduring legacy in modern art.

Jeff Koons

Jeff Koons (born 1955) is an American artist known for his work in contemporary art. His sculpture "Balloon Venus" reflects his fascination with popular culture and consumerism, featuring glossy, balloon-like forms that challenge traditional notions of art and beauty. Koons' work is often characterized by its playful and provocative nature.

Johannes Vermeer

Johannes Vermeer (1632-1675) was a Dutch Baroque painter renowned for his delicate and meticulously detailed work. His painting "Girl with a Pearl Earring" is one of his most famous pieces, celebrated for its captivating portrayal of a young woman and masterful use of light and shadow. Vermeer's paintings are revered for their intimate and realistic depiction of domestic life.

M F Hussain

M F Hussain (1915-2011) was an influential Indian painter known for his vibrant and dynamic works that often explored themes of Indian culture and history. His painting "Mehendi" is an example of his use of bold colors and expressive forms to celebrate traditional art forms and cultural narratives. Hussain’s work remains a significant part of Indian modern art.

Sita Devi

Sita Devi (born 1940) is a prominent artist known for her contributions to the Madhubani painting style, a traditional Indian art form characterized by intricate patterns and vibrant colors. Her "Madhubani Painting" showcases her skill in depicting mythological and folk themes through this unique and culturally rich artistic tradition.
